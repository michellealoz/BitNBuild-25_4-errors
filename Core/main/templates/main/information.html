The Foundation of Wellness

Comprehensive Ayurvedic Glossary

A detailed guide to the fundamental concepts that govern health, balance, and healing in the ancient science of Ayurveda.

Tridoshas (Three Fundamental Energies)

These are the three biological energies that govern the body and mind

Vata Dosha

Elements: Air + Ether (Space)

Governs all movement, communication, and the nervous system. Its qualities are dry, light, cold, mobile, and rough.

Pitta Dosha

Elements: Fire + Water

Governs metabolism, digestion, and transformation. Its qualities are hot, sharp, oily, light, and intense.

Kapha Dosha

Elements: Earth + Water

Governs structure, stability, and lubrication. Its qualities are heavy, slow, steady, solid, oily, and cool.

Panchamahabhutas (Five Great Elements)

The five fundamental elements of nature.

Akasha

Ether / Space

Vayu

Air

Agni

Fire

Jala / Apas

Water

Prithvi

Earth

Shad Rasa

The six basic tastes recognized in Ayurveda.

Madhura (Sweet)

Elements: Earth + Water

Builds tissue, grounding. Found in grains, sweet fruits, dairy.

Amla (Sour)

Elements: Earth + Fire

Cleansing, stimulating for digestion. Found in citrus fruits, fermented foods.

Lavana (Salty)

Elements: Water + Fire

Retains water, moistening. Found in salt, seaweed, processed foods.

Katu (Pungent)

Elements: Fire + Air

Clears sinuses, stimulates Agni. Found in chili peppers, garlic, ginger.

Tikta (Bitter)

Elements: Air + Ether

Lightens, detoxifies, cools. Found in leafy greens, turmeric, bitter melon.

Kashaya (Astringent)

Elements: Air + Earth

Drying, constricting. Found in legumes, pomegranate, green tea.

Gunas (20 Qualities)

These are paired opposing qualities that describe everything in the universe, from food to personality traits. Balancing them is key to health.

Guru (Heavy) vs. Laghu (Light)
Manda (Dull/Slow) vs. Tikshna (Sharp)
Hima (Cold) vs. Ushna (Hot)
Snigdha (Oily) vs. Ruksha (Dry)
Shlakshna (Smooth) vs. Khara (Rough)
Sandra (Dense) vs. Drava (Liquid)
Mrudu (Soft) vs. Kathina (Hard)
Sthira (Stable) vs. Chala (Mobile)
Sthula (Gross) vs. Sukshma (Subtle)
Picchila (Sticky) vs. Vishada (Clear)

Other Key Concepts

Sapta Dhatus

The seven bodily tissues that build and nourish the body: Rasa (Plasma), Rakta (Blood), Mamsa (Muscle), Meda (Fat), Asthi (Bone), Majja (Bone marrow), and Shukra/Artava (Reproductive tissue).

Malas

The body's three main waste products: Purisha (Feces), Mutra (Urine), and Sveda (Sweat). Proper elimination is crucial for health.

Agni

The "digestive fire" responsible for all metabolic processes, including the digestion of food, thoughts, and emotions. A strong Agni is essential for health.

Srotas

The channels or pathways of the body that transport nutrients, waste, and energy. Blockages in the Srotas lead to disease.

Manas

The mind, which is influenced by the mental qualities of Sattva (purity), Rajas (activity), and Tamas (inertia). A balanced mind is key to physical health.

Prakriti & Vikriti

Prakriti is one's unique, permanent constitution at birth. Vikriti is the current state of imbalance, which can be corrected with lifestyle and diet changes.

Dinacharya & Ritucharya

Dinacharya is the daily routine aligned with natural rhythms. Ritucharya is the seasonal routine, both of which are essential for maintaining balance.

Ojas, Tejas, Prana

The three vital life energies. Ojas is vital essence/immunity. Tejas is inner fire/radiance. Prana is the life force that flows throughout the body.
